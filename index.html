COVID-19 and Healthcare Workers

On March 13th 2020 a public health emergency was declared by President Trump.
It led to wide-ranging shocks to the labor market.
Data shown as a percentage change relative to January 2020 levels
The healthcare industry was no exception.
But as COVID-19 progressed, the economy and the overall healthcare industry recovered.
Disaggregating the healthcare industry tells a slightly different story. This line is for ambulatory care which includes doctor's offices, dentists, and optometrists. It still mirrors the general trend seen in the total US labor market.
In contrast, hospital employment did not drop as dramatically between March and April 2020, but has decreased overall compared to January 2020.
Another outlier is nursing and residential care: employment shows a clear decreasing trend as the pandemic has progressed and has not recoverd.
